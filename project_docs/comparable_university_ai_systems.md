# Universities Using Similar AI Assistants

## Arizona State University

ASU provides ChatGPT Edu through an enterprise agreement and states that ASU data is stored safely and not used to train OpenAI models. ASU's CreateAI Chat is also positioned as a secure assistant built for ASU, with document chat, model choice, and institutional governance.

Lesson for Eve: ESUI needs governance, privacy, approved data sources, and clear acceptable-use boundaries.

## University of Michigan

The University of Michigan offers U-M GPT and U-M Maizey to faculty, staff, and students. Maizey allows users to enrich the AI experience using custom datasets. U-M also provides Go Blue, a mobile AI campus companion with campus information, student support, accessibility, and future data-source expansion.

Lesson for Eve: Eve should become a campus companion, not just a Q&A page. Future versions should support mobile access, feedback, LMS integration, and controlled institutional datasets.

## Georgia State University

Georgia State's Pounce chatbot has been used for student-success support. A 2022 Georgia State report describes improved student performance when course-related chatbot messages were used, including higher rates of B-or-above grades and stronger performance among first-generation students.

Lesson for Eve: proactive academic nudges, reminders, and practice quizzes can be part of the student success module.

## University of Houston

The University of Houston's Shasta chatbot is deployed across departmental websites, uses generative AI based on departmental website content, and is managed with customer-service oversight. UH reported thousands of chats and a high resolution rate.

Lesson for Eve: each ESUI unit should own and review its knowledge content; the AI system should include analytics and human oversight.

## University of Alaska Fairbanks

UAF launched an Ocelot chatbot with preloaded office information, generative AI crawling of UAF website content, live assistant handoff, text campaigns, and planned integration with student account systems.

Lesson for Eve: a phased roadmap is realistic: public knowledge first, then student system integration, then live handoff and proactive support.
