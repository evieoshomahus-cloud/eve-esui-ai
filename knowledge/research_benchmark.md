# Comparable University AI Systems

These examples inform Eve's design direction.

| Institution | System | What Eve Should Learn |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Arizona State University | ChatGPT Edu and CreateAI Chat | Enterprise AI should be governed, privacy-aware, connected to institutional knowledge, and available to students/staff through secure access. |
| University of Michigan | U-M GPT, Maizey, MiMaizey, Go Blue | A serious university AI ecosystem can include custom datasets, a mobile campus assistant, privacy assurances, accessibility, voice/image features, and feedback loops. |
| Georgia State University | Pounce / PolsPounce | AI support can improve student performance when it sends targeted academic nudges and connects students to support at the right time. |
| University of Houston | Shasta | Departmental AI assistants should be managed collaboratively with service teams, website content owners, and analytics review. |
| University of Alaska Fairbanks | Ocelot chatbot | Student-facing chatbots can combine preloaded office information, generative search over university pages, live assistant handoff, and future integration with student systems. |

Design implication: Eve should not be presented as a generic chatbot. It should be designed as an institutional AI layer with RAG, role-based data access, audit logs, feedback, and future integrations with admissions, LMS, finance, and student records.
